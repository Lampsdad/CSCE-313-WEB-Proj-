About Me

Howdy! My name is Keith Crabb, I am a Junior at Texas A&M University, Majoring in Computing with a minor in cybersecurity.

My passion for computing started when I was little, helping my father around the Data Center at the University of Houston. I was the unofficial “data center rat,” as I was small enough to crawl around underneath the floorboards and run wires between the different server racks.

Starting High School, however, is really when my interest in computing and cybersecurity began taking off. I was lent a school laptop to help with my studies and immediately began exploring the system and its capabilities. Instead of paying attention during music theory, I was in the back of the room researching the basics of coding with C++ and researching how the internet was networked.

Over the years, I explored every nook and cranny of my school’s systems and pushed the limits of the equipment I was given. As I learned new skills, I began to teach others around me, turning those I taught into teammates, whom I led into new projects and exciting unexplored areas.

During my time at college, I ran across the Scholarship for Service Cybercorps scholarship, which is a program run by the National Science Foundation and Department of Homeland Security to attract and train the next generation of Government Cyber Professionals.

A few weeks after I applied, exhausted from staying up all night working on a final project, I arrived at my interview at the wrong time on the wrong day. Despite this, the program director made time for me, and a couple of days later, he reached out, letting me know that I had been accepted into the program. Since then, I’ve been admitted into a few other scholarship programs that are similar in nature and provide mentorship and professional development experiences that have helped me narrow my focus and prepare for a career.
